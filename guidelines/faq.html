Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about contributing to CU Archive

Common Questions & Answers

1
Do I need to create an account to contribute?

No, you don't need to create an account. You can submit papers anonymously or provide your name for attribution. We only require basic information about the paper you're submitting.

When you submit a paper:

  • Name field is optional: Leave it blank to remain anonymous
  • Email field is optional: Provide it only if you want status updates
  • No login required: Submit directly through our contribution form
  • No password to remember: Simple one-time submission process

This makes contributing quick and accessible for everyone while maintaining your privacy.

2
How long does it take for my submission to be reviewed?

Typically 3-7 days, but it can vary based on several factors:

  • Standard timeline: 3-7 business days for most submissions
  • Peak periods: Up to 2 weeks during exam seasons (May-June, Nov-Dec)
  • Complex submissions: Longer if additional verification is needed
  • Weekends/holidays: Review team may not be available

What affects review time:

  1. Quality of submission (clear, complete submissions are reviewed faster)
  2. Volume of submissions (more submissions = longer queues)
  3. Time of year (exam seasons are busiest)
  4. Completeness of information (complete metadata speeds up review)

Notification: If you provide an email address, we'll notify you once your submission is approved or if we need additional information.

3
Can I submit papers from previous universities or other institutions?

Our primary focus is University of Calcutta papers, but we may accept papers from:

  • Affiliated colleges: Colleges officially affiliated with University of Calcutta
  • Similar courses: Papers from other universities with equivalent courses
  • Relevant content: Papers that would benefit University of Calcutta students

Please ensure:

  1. The paper is relevant to courses we support (BSc, BCom, BA, and related programs)
  2. You clearly indicate the source institution in the "Additional Notes" field
  3. The paper follows all our quality standards and format requirements
  4. You have the right to share the paper (no copyright violations)

Note: Papers from completely unrelated institutions or courses may be rejected if they don't serve our primary audience.

4
What if I don't have a digital copy, only a physical paper?

You can digitize physical papers using several methods:

Option 1: Smartphone Scanning Apps

  • Google Drive: Has a built-in document scanner
  • Adobe Scan: Free app that converts photos to PDF
  • Microsoft Office Lens: Great for document scanning
  • CamScanner: Popular scanning app (free version available)

Option 2: Traditional Scanner

  • Scan at 300 DPI or higher for best quality
  • Save as PDF format
  • Use black and white or grayscale for text documents

Option 3: Library/Print Shop Services

  • Many libraries offer free scanning services
  • Print shops can scan documents for a small fee
  • University computer labs often have scanners

Tips for good results:

  • Ensure even lighting with no shadows or glare
  • Place paper on a dark, contrasting surface
  • Hold camera directly above the paper (not at an angle)
  • Make sure entire page is in frame
  • Check that all text is legible before submitting
5
Can I submit multiple papers at once?

Yes, but each paper must be submitted separately. Here's why and how:

Why Separate Submissions?

  • Proper categorization: Each paper needs its own metadata (course, year, subject)
  • Individual review: Each paper is reviewed independently for quality
  • Better organization: Separate submissions make the archive more searchable
  • Faster processing: Bulk submissions often get stuck in review

How to Submit Multiple Papers Efficiently:

  1. Prepare all files first: Ensure each is properly named and in PDF format
  2. Use the same browser session: Your form data may autofill for subsequent submissions
  3. Submit systematically: Complete one submission before starting the next
  4. Keep track: Note which papers you've submitted to avoid duplicates

What NOT to Do:

  • Don't upload zip/rar files: They will be rejected
  • Don't combine multiple papers into one PDF: Each paper should be separate
  • Don't rush: Take time to provide accurate information for each submission

Tip: If you have a large collection (10+ papers), consider contacting us first at contributions@cuarchive.example.com for guidance.

6
What happens if my submission is rejected?

If your submission is rejected, you'll receive an email (if provided) explaining the reason. Here's what happens and what you can do:

Rejection Process:

  1. Notification: Email sent explaining specific reason(s) for rejection
  2. Feedback: Clear instructions on what needs to be fixed
  3. No penalty: Rejection doesn't affect future submissions
  4. Opportunity to resubmit: You can fix issues and submit again

Common Reasons for Rejection:

  • Poor quality scan: Blurry, dark, or illegible text
  • Incorrect format: Not PDF, or wrong file type for question papers
  • Missing information: Incomplete metadata (course, year, subject)
  • Duplicate: Paper already exists in archive
  • Incomplete paper: Missing pages or sections
  • Wrong content: Not a question paper, or contains answer key

How to Resubmit After Rejection:

  1. Read the rejection email carefully to understand the specific issues
  2. Fix all mentioned problems before resubmitting
  3. Use the same contribution form to submit again
  4. Consider adding a note in "Additional Information" explaining corrections made

Important: There's no limit on resubmissions, but please ensure you address all mentioned issues before resubmitting to avoid repeated rejections.

7
How are contributors credited?

We offer flexible attribution options to respect your privacy while recognizing your contribution.

Attribution Options:

  • Full Attribution: "Contributed by [Your Full Name]" displayed on paper's download page
  • Partial Attribution: "Contributed by [First Name]" or initials
  • Anonymous: No name displayed (simply shows "Contributed by a student")
  • Pseudonym: Use a nickname or username instead of real name

Where Attribution Appears:

  1. Paper Download Page: Below the paper details
  2. Search Results: In paper metadata (if enabled)
  3. Contributors Page: List of all contributors (optional)
  4. Monthly Newsletter: Feature top contributors (optional)

Additional Recognition:

  • Top Contributors: Featured on our contributors page
  • Special Thanks: Annual acknowledgment of major contributors
  • Social Media Shoutouts: With permission, we may feature contributors on our social media

Changing Attribution Later:

If you want to change how you're credited after submission:

  1. From named to anonymous: Email us at contributions@cuarchive.example.com with the paper details
  2. From anonymous to named: Contact us with proof of contribution
  3. Name correction: We can update your name if there was a typo

Privacy First: Your choice of attribution is always respected. We never share email addresses or other contact information publicly.

8
Can I update or remove a paper I've submitted?

Yes, but with some limitations. Here's what you can and cannot do:

What You CAN Do:

  • Update metadata: Fix errors in course, year, subject information
  • Change attribution: Update your name or switch to anonymous
  • Replace file: Upload a better quality version of the same paper
  • Add information: Provide additional details in notes section

What You CANNOT Do:

  • Remove published papers easily: Once published, papers generally remain in archive
  • Change to different paper: Cannot replace one paper with a completely different one
  • Bypass review: Updates still go through review process

How to Request Updates/Removal:

  1. Email us: Contact contributions@cuarchive.example.com
  2. Include details: Paper title, submission date, your email (if used)
  3. Explain request: Clearly state what needs to be changed and why
  4. Provide proof: If requesting removal, explain the reason

Removal Circumstances:

Papers may be removed in these cases:

  • Copyright claim: Legitimate copyright holder requests removal
  • Incorrect paper: Wrong paper was submitted accidentally
  • Privacy concern: Personal information is included in the paper
  • Duplicate: Exact duplicate of another paper in archive

Note: We aim to preserve the educational archive, so removal is considered carefully. Updates are generally easier and faster than removals.

9
Are there any restrictions on the age of papers I can submit?

No, we accept papers from any year! Older papers are often particularly valuable for several reasons:

Why Older Papers Are Valuable:

  • Historical perspective: Shows how subjects and examination patterns have evolved
  • Research value: Useful for academic research on education trends
  • Comparative studies: Helps students understand changes in curriculum
  • Rare finds: Older papers are often harder to find, making them more valuable
  • Comprehensive archive: A complete historical record benefits everyone

Special Considerations for Older Papers:

  1. Scan quality: Older papers may need careful scanning due to paper condition
  2. Metadata accuracy: Ensure course/subject names are correct for the time period
  3. Legibility: Faded ink or poor paper quality may affect readability
  4. Historical context: Add notes about any historical significance in "Additional Notes"

Age Categories:

  • Recent (0-5 years): Most relevant for current students
  • Mid-range (6-15 years): Shows recent curriculum changes
  • Historical (16+ years): Valuable for research and historical perspective
  • Very old (pre-2000): Rare and particularly valuable for archive completeness

Digitizing Old Papers:

If you have fragile or delicate old papers:

  • Handle carefully: Avoid further damage during scanning
  • Professional help: Consider professional scanning for very old/fragile papers
  • Multiple angles: Take photos from different angles if paper is curled or damaged
  • Note condition: Mention paper condition in submission notes

Encouragement: We especially welcome submissions of older papers, as they help create a more comprehensive historical archive for researchers and students alike.

10
What if I'm not sure about the course or subject of a paper?

It's better to submit with partial information than not at all! Here's what to do when you're uncertain:

When You're Unsure:

  1. Provide what you know: Fill in the fields you're certain about
  2. Use "Additional Notes": Explain your uncertainties here
  3. Make educated guesses: Use your best judgment for uncertain fields
  4. Indicate uncertainty: Add question marks or "possibly" in uncertain fields

Specific Guidance:

  • Course unsure: Select "Other Course" and explain in notes
  • Year unsure: Make your best estimate based on paper style/content
  • Subject unsure: List possible subjects separated by slashes (e.g., "Physics/Chemistry")
  • Semester unsure: Leave blank if completely unknown

What Our Review Team Does:

When we receive submissions with uncertain information:

  1. Research: We try to verify information based on paper content
  2. Contact you: If email provided, we may ask for clarification
  3. Expert consultation: We consult subject experts when needed
  4. Make best determination: Based on available information

Examples of How to Handle Uncertainty:

  • Instead of leaving blank: "BSc (possibly)" in course field
  • Instead of guessing: "Mathematics or Statistics" in subject field
  • In notes field: "Found this paper in old notes, not sure of exact year but appears to be from 2015-2018 based on question style"
  • When completely unsure: "Unknown course - appears to be science related based on content"

Important: It's always better to submit a paper with some uncertainty than to not submit it at all. Many students may still find it useful even with incomplete metadata, and our review team can often help fill in the gaps.
