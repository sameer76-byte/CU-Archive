Quality Standards

Understand our quality requirements for submissions to ensure your contributions are approved

Content Quality Requirements

To ensure our archive maintains high educational value, all submissions must meet these quality standards. Submissions that don't meet these requirements will be rejected.

Legibility

Critical Requirement: All text must be clearly readable. Blurry, pixelated, or low-resolution scans will be rejected immediately.

  • Minimum 300 DPI for scanned documents
  • Proper contrast between text and background
  • No shadows, glare, or distortions

Completeness

Critical Requirement: Submit complete question papers only. Missing pages, cropped content, or incomplete sections will result in rejection.

  • All pages must be included
  • No missing questions or sections
  • Full paper from cover to end

Accuracy

Important Requirement: Information about course, year, subject, and semester must be accurate. Mislabeled papers confuse users and waste archive space.

  • Verify course and year information
  • Double-check subject name
  • Confirm semester if applicable

Relevance

Important Requirement: Only academic question papers are accepted. Non-academic content, personal notes, or unrelated materials will be rejected.

  • University/college question papers only
  • No answer keys or solved papers
  • No personal or private documents

Document Preparation Guidelines

Follow these guidelines when preparing your documents for submission to ensure they meet our quality standards.

For Scanned Documents

Resolution

Scan at a minimum of 300 DPI (dots per inch) for optimal clarity. Higher resolutions (600 DPI) are better for detailed diagrams.

Alignment

Ensure proper alignment and orientation. Crooked or skewed scans are difficult to read and may be rejected.

Color Mode

Use black and white or grayscale for text documents. Color mode increases file size without improving readability for most papers.

Lighting

Remove any shadows, glare, or distortions. Ensure even lighting when photographing or scanning papers.

For Digital Documents

Print Quality

Save as "Print Quality" PDF to ensure sharp text and proper formatting for printing if needed.

Font Embedding

Include all fonts embedded in the PDF to ensure consistent display across different devices and operating systems.

Security

Remove any password protection or restrictions. Protected files cannot be reviewed or processed.

Hyperlinks

Ensure hyperlinks are functional (if applicable). Broken links detract from document quality.

Review Process

All submissions go through a comprehensive three-step review process. Understanding this process helps you prepare better submissions.

1
Automated Check

Our system automatically verifies:

  • File format compatibility
  • File size limits (max 10MB for PDFs)
  • Basic metadata completeness
  • Virus/malware scanning

Time: Instant

2
Content Review

Our volunteer review team manually checks:

  • Document quality and legibility
  • Content relevance and completeness
  • Accuracy of metadata (course, year, subject)
  • Check for duplicates in archive

Time: 3-7 days (longer during peak periods)

3
Final Approval

Quality assurance before publishing:

  • Final verification of all requirements
  • Proper categorization and tagging
  • Addition to search index
  • Notification to contributor (if email provided)

Time: 1-2 days after content review

Common Reasons for Rejection

Understanding why submissions are rejected helps you avoid common mistakes. Here are the most frequent reasons for rejection:

Reason for Rejection Frequency How to Avoid Status
Poor scan quality (blurry, dark, skewed) High Scan at 300+ DPI, ensure good lighting Rejected
Incorrect metadata (wrong course, year, subject) High Double-check all information before submitting Rejected
Duplicate paper (already in archive) Medium Search archive thoroughly before submitting Rejected
Incomplete paper (missing pages) Medium Verify all pages are included and in order Rejected
Wrong file format (not PDF for question papers) Low Convert to PDF before submission Rejected
Non-academic content Low Submit only university question papers Rejected

Resubmission After Rejection

If your submission is rejected, you'll receive an email (if provided) explaining the reason. You can correct the issues and resubmit. There's no limit on resubmissions, but please ensure you address all mentioned issues before resubmitting.
